Public Transport Modernization

The public transport network modernization aims to improve route efficiency, reduce travel times, and promote eco-friendly commuting. By applying BFS (Breadth-First Search) and DFS (Depth-First Search) algorithms, we can analyze and optimize transportation routes. BFS ensures that all nearby stops are explored systematically, enabling efficient travel planning and real-time tracking. DFS helps identify isolated or less connected stops and assesses deeper route structures for possible detours.

This modernization ensures faster connectivity between stops, better resource allocation, and reduced environmental impact through optimized routes, benefiting both the commuters and the environment.
